# Go-To-Market (GTM)

## Current framing
This document tracks positioning, target users, messaging, launch strategy, and go-to-market risks for MyGym / repEtition.

## Website signal
Current site headline and feature language suggest this positioning:
- workout tracker for serious lifters
- AI-powered programming
- local-first data
- zero fluff

## Hypotheses to validate
- serious lifters may respond to speed, clarity, and practical utility over “community” features
- local-first / no-account positioning may be a differentiator
- AI programming may be a hook, but only if the value is immediately understandable
- the app may currently be more intuitive to China than to first-time users

## GTM workstreams
- Ideal customer profile (ICP)
- positioning and differentiation
- onboarding clarity
- launch sequence
- pricing / monetization
- landing page and messaging
- objection handling
- feedback loops from early users

## Questions parked for later
- Who is the best first user?
- What is the sharpest value proposition?
- Is the AI angle the wedge, or the tracker UX itself?
- Should launch start with private users, beta users, or broader public access?
- What proof points are needed before pushing harder?
